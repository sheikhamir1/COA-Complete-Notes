What is Boolean Algebra?

Boolean algebra is a branch of mathematics that works with only two values:

  • 0 ‚Üí False (OFF, Low voltage) üîå
  • 1 ‚Üí True (ON, High voltage) üí°

It was introduced by George Boole in the 19th century.

In computers, Boolean algebra is the foundation of digital logic (how circuits and processors work). üîß

Example:

A simple light switch:

  • Switch OFF = 0
  • Switch ON = 1

If we say Light = Switch1 AND Switch2, the light will only turn ON if both switches are ON. üí°

Boolean Laws and Theorems - Part 1

These laws are like the rules of arithmetic, but for logic. They make it possible to simplify complex logical circuits. ‚öôÔ∏è

Commutative Laws

Order doesn't matter.

OR: A + B = B + A

AND: AB = BA

Example:

Pressing Switch A OR B will turn ON the fan, no matter the order. üåÄ

Associative Laws

Grouping doesn't matter.

OR: (A + B) + C = A + (B + C)

AND: (AB)C = A(BC)

Example:

If 3 people vote YES, the result doesn't depend on the grouping order. üë•

Distributive Laws

Just like in normal algebra, but with AND/OR.

OR distributes over AND: A(B + C) = AB + AC

AND distributes over OR: A + BC = (A + B)(A + C)

Example:

A(B + C) ‚Üí If A must be ON and either B or C is ON ‚Üí it's same as (A and B) OR (A and C). üí°
